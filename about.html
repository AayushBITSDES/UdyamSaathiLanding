Building voice-based scheme discovery infrastructure for India's micro-enterprises

Project Overview

Udyam Saathi is an early-stage project focused on building voice-based scheme discovery infrastructure for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in India. Our mission is to bridge the gap between government schemes and the businesses that need them most.

We are developing an AI-powered voice-first assistant that helps India's informal businesses discover and understand government schemes in their preferred language. By leveraging Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) technology and natural language processing, we aim to make scheme discovery as simple as having a conversation.

This project recognizes that traditional text-based portals often exclude millions of micro-enterprise owners who may not be comfortable with written English or digital interfaces. Our voice-first approach ensures that language and literacy barriers don't prevent access to valuable government support programs.

Currently in prototype phase, we're focused on building core capabilities including scheme matching algorithms, multilingual voice interfaces, and conversation management systems that can guide users through the complex landscape of government schemes.
